Vitamins and Minerals

What are Vitamins and Minerals

Vitamins and minerals are known as micronutrients and are essential elements of normal bodily function. Minerals, like iron or calcium are things are body needs that it cannot produce on its own.Similarly vitamins are almost only derived from our food directly and cannot be produced by our body( this isnt the case for vitamin D, of which we can absorb from sunlight on the skin ). A lack of or "deficiency" in minerals or vitamins can lead to health problems, so hopefully its clear that we should have them in our minds when trying to keep our bodies in tip top shape.

Vitamins and Minerals your body needs

I must point out that there are to distinct classes of vitamins that our relevant, water-soluble and fat-soluble. This doesn't come up much however it's important to note that water-soluble vitamins are excreted from the body if not used, while fat-soluble vitamins can build up. I will now list all of the important vitamins your body needs, what foods to get them from, and the effect a deficiency could have on your body:

List of Vitamins

  • Vitamin A --Fat soluble;
    • Essential for your bodies metabolism and cell growth
    • Particularly noticable symptoms of a deficiency arise in the eyes,they can become dry, the sclera can form dark blotches and partial blindness can ensue
    • Good sources of vitamin A include, meats( in general ) and many vegetables, namely cooked sweet potatoes being a great source
  • Vitamin B12 --Water soluble;
    • Notably imortant in producing healthy red blood cells
    • A deficiency can cause anemia( reduction of haemoglobin, oxhagen carrying cells )
    • Good sources of vitamin b12 include, red meats and dairy products --if you are vegan many vegan milks add vitamin b12 , just be sure to check the label
  • Vitamin C --Water soluble;
    • Important for soft tissue growth and repair
    • A deficiency can cause scurvy
    • Good sources of vitamin C are all fruits and vegetables really
  • Vitamin D --Fat soluble;
    • Presence of vitamin D allows the body to absorb calcium( mineral used to make bone tissue )
    • A deficiency can cause osteomalacia( causing the bones to soften and become weaker )
    • Good sources of vitamin D are sunlight, some supplemented dairy products and vitamin D suplements themselves
  • Vitamin E --Fat soluble;
    • Important for the immune system as well as tissue growth
    • A deficiency can cause digestive issues, however it is very rare and not usually caused by a lack of vitamin E
    • Good sources include plant based oils and and nuts

Below I have listed and touched on the most important minerals, you should ensure to incorporate foods high in these minerals into your diet:

List of Minerals

  • Iron;
    • Important for production of hemoglobin, deficiency can cause anemia
    • Can be found in red meats and spinach
  • Calcium;
    • Important for healthy bones, deficiency can cause osteomalacia and osteoporosous( bone structure becomes more porous )
    • Can be found in dairy products, legumes and calcium-fortified milk( vegan )
